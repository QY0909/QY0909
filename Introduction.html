INTRODUCTION TO QWERTY AND COLEMAK KEYBOARD

QWERTY

A keyboard layout for latin-script alphabets is qwerty. The name is derived from the placement of the first six keys on the keyboard's upper-left letter row (Q W E R T Y). Based on a layout developed for the sholes and glidden typewriter and sold to e. Remington and sons in 1873, the qwerty design was created. Christopher Latham Sholes conceptualized and developed the qwerty layout in the early 1870s.

COLEMAK

The most commonly used English-language letters are located on the primary row in the keyboard layout known as Colemak, which is for Latin-script alphabets. This arrangement is intended to make typing more pleasant and fast. It was established on 1 January 2006, and is named for Shai Coleman, the person who established it.

Founded 1 January 2006
